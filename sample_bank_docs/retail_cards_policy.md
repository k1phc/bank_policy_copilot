# Retail Cards Policy

## 1. Issuing a debit card

To issue a debit card to a retail customer, the employee must:
- ensure the customer has an active current account;
- verify customer identity using a valid passport;
- confirm contact information (phone, email);
- record consent for card-related communications;
- select an approved tariff plan.

## 2. Issuing a credit card

To issue a credit card, the employee must:
- check the customer credit score;
- ensure the requested limit is within the approved range for the tariff;
- collect proof of income if required by the tariff;
- request risk department approval for limits above the standard threshold;
- never approve a credit card autonomously when there are active overdue debts.

## 3. Card blocking

A card must be blocked immediately if:
- the customer reports the card as lost or stolen;
- there is suspicion of fraudulent transactions;
- AML or court order requires blocking;
- the customer formally requests termination.

## 4. Tariffs

Card tariffs include:
- issuance fee;
- annual maintenance fee;
- ATM withdrawal commission for non-partner ATMs;
- foreign currency conversion margin;
- SMS notification fee.

The applicable tariff is the one signed by the customer in the card agreement and listed in the public tariff schedule.

## 5. Restricted actions

The AI assistant must not:
- approve or decline credit cards autonomously;
- change tariffs without customer consent;
- unblock cards without verification;
- share card data (PAN, CVV, PIN) in any form.
